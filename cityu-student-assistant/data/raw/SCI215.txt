Course Code: SCI215
Title: SCI 215 Environmental Science (NS)
URL: https://cityu.smartcatalogiq.com/en/2025-2026/2025-2026-catalog/course-descriptions/sci-science/200/sci-215
Credits: 5
Prerequisites: None

Description:
This course explores environmental change on a local, national, and global scale while emphasizing the fundamental concepts of ecology and conservationism. An understanding of environmental issues can be applied to emerging trends toward sustainability, renewable energy, and reduction of waste in professional fields. The ethical considerations involved in environmental legislation and policy decision-making that aim to mitigate environmental issues are also examined.

Outcomes:
This course will prepare students to:
1. Explain historical and environmental impacts on the earth, including scientific phenomena and human influence. (Environmental Challenges Persuasive Presentation)
2. Reflect on diverse perspectives and solutions to contemporary environmental issues. (Environmental Challenges Persuasive Presentation, Environmental Impact Paper)
3. Summarize ethical considerations involved in environmental legislation and policy decision-making on local and global levels. (Environmental Impact Paper)

Course Code: EEA644
Title: EEA 644 Leadership Internship III *
URL: https://cityu.smartcatalogiq.com/en/2025-2026/2025-2026-catalog/course-descriptions/eea-educational-administration/600/eea-644
Credits: 3
Prerequisites: EEA 641; EEA 642

Description:
Students are mentored, integrated, and involved in this sequential field experience as planned by the student, the university field supervisor, and the district mentor. The student applies theory to the real life of the school while learning the leadership roles and responsibilities of the principal, curriculum leader or other school leader. Internship III engages the student in applying and practicing the entry level knowledge, skills, and dispositions of educational leaders at the school and district levels. Students build capacity for leadership through activities in the field assigned by the mentor and feedback on those activities. The student maintains a daily log and weekly reflection and assesses progress toward mastery of entry level knowledge and skills.

Outcomes:
This course will prepare students to:
1. Demonstrate visionary and strategic leadership.
2. Utilize assessment and appraisal techniques for students and staff in school settings.
3. Apply significant data driven research to develop and evaluate strategies and/or systems to support a educational issue/project relevant to leadership and continuous school improvement.
4. Analyze self within the concepts of reflection and personal professional growth.
5. Analyze the roles and responsibilities of the school leaders.
6. Develop with staff, students, families, and community in the implementation of educational initiatives and continuous school improvement that includes indigenous and sustainability perspectives.

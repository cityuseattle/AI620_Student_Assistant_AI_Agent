Course Code: EEA532
Title: EEA 532 Instructional Leadership: Curriculum, Instruction and Assessment
URL: https://cityu.smartcatalogiq.com/en/2025-2026/2025-2026-catalog/course-descriptions/eea-educational-administration/500/eea-532
Credits: 3
Prerequisites: None

Description:
Students gain applicable knowledge and skills that lead to an understanding of how school leaders manage instructional processes. Students learn about a leader’s view of curriculum, instruction and assessment, and pedagogy that guide teachers along the career continuum. Students build leadership capacity to make a positive impact on teaching, learning, and assessment, as well as review of provincial and territorial government documents.

Outcomes:
This course prepares students to:
1. Examine social responsibility and sustainability as they are found within the provincial/territorial curriculum, assessment, and instruction documents.
2. Evaluate instructional pedagogy utilizing evidence and field-based educational research.
3. Evaluate how teaching pedagogy is used to guide provincial/territorial and professional curriculum standards.
4. Create effective teaching methodologies for school-wide curriculum, assessment, and instructional practices.
5. Design Indigenous approaches to curriculum, assessment, and instructional practices.

Course Code: SOC412
Title: SOC 412 Sociology of the Family (SS)
URL: https://cityu.smartcatalogiq.com/en/2025-2026/2025-2026-catalog/course-descriptions/soc-sociology/400/soc-412
Credits: 5
Prerequisites: None

Description:
This course will cover five broad but related areas of study: (1) the origins of the family, as well as basic concepts and theories underlying sociological discussions of family; (2) the relationship between the family and society through history; (3) work roles for men and women, both within and outside the home; (4) trends in non-marital lifestyles, marriage, divorce, and childbearing; and (5) the outlook for the future of the family.

Outcomes:
This course will prepare students to:
Discuss various challenges facing contemporary families.
Differentiate between various definitions of “family”, various family systems, and structures throughout history.
Assess the division of labor between men and women within and outside the home.
Evaluate factors that influence trends in non-marital lifestyles, marriage, divorce, remarriage, and childbearing.
Evaluate the relationships between love, sex, and marriage.
Appraise various methodological and theoretical approaches to studying marriage and family.

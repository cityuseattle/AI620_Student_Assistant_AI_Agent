Course Code: COUN526
Title: COUN 526 Counseling Theory
URL: https://cityu.smartcatalogiq.com/en/2025-2026/2025-2026-catalog/course-descriptions/coun-counseling/500/coun-526
Credits: 5
Prerequisites: None

Description:
This course provides a graduate-level introduction to counseling theories and their practical application during the counseling process. Students are introduced to the five major theoretical schools (psychodynamic, humanistic, behavioral, systemic, and constructivist). The course also explores a number of current issues in counseling and psychotherapy. Each student deeply analyzes their beliefs about human behavior, resulting in the development of an initial, critically-informed personal theory of counseling.

Outcomes:
This course will prepare students to:
1. Apply theories and models of counseling to client case scenarios.
2. Develop an initial personal model of counseling.
3. Develop self-awareness into beliefs about what impacts personal development, and formulate a personal philosophy of counseling grounded in these beliefs.
